The Internet of Things (IoT) has revolutionized how we interact with the world, generating vast amounts of data from connected devices. To effectively manage and analyze this data, specialized data engineering techniques are essential. In this workshop, we will delve into the realm of IoT data engineering, focusing on constructing robust data pipelines capable of tackling the unique challenges <<<<<<< HEAD posed by IoT data. PC CHAIRS

Takoua Abdellatif is a highly accomplished professional in the field of IT engineering. She earned her IT engineering degree from the prestigious ENSIMAG Engineering School in Grenoble, France, in 1998. Following her passion for research, she pursued a Ph.D. degree at INRIA, specializing in autonomic management distributed systems, and successfully obtained her doctorate in 2006. With a strong foundation in academia and research, Takoua ventured into industry as a Research and Development Engineer at Hewlett Packard in Grenoble. Her work focused on the HP Telecom platform, where she made significant contributions for a duration of five years. Her expertise in developing and optimizing telecom systems greatly benefited the company. Continuing her career in research, joined Bull/Grenoble as a Research Engineer. In this role, she dedicated her efforts to enhancing systems' scalability and fault-tolerance, contributing to the advancement of reliable and high-performing technologies. Currently, Takoua Abdellatif serves as an Associate Professor in IT at Sousse University and Polytechnics School of Tunisia (EPT), which is affiliated with Carthage University. Alongside her teaching responsibilities, she holds the position of Senior Researcher at the EPT/SERCOM Research Laboratory. As the coordinator of the lab's research activities focused on scalable and secure distributed systems, she plays a pivotal role in driving innovative research and fostering collaboration within the field.

Abderrazak Jemai is a Professor at National Institute of Applied Science and Technology (INSAT), University of Carthage, Tunis, Tunisia. He received an Engineer degree from the University of Tunis (ENSI), Tunisia in 1988 and the DEA and "Doctor" degrees from the University of Grenoble (ENSIMAG-INPG), France, in 1989 and 1992, respectively, all in computer science. He became Full Professor in computer Science in July 2018. From 1989 to 1992, he prepared his thesis on simulation of RISC processors and parallel architectures. Since 1993, his interests are focused on high-level synthesis and simulation at behavioral and system levels within AMICAL and COSMOS at TIMA Laboratory in Grenoble. Abderrazak Jemai became an Assistant at the ENSI University in Tunis in 1993 and an Assistant-Professor at the INSAT University in Tunis from 1994 to 2013. In 2013, he became an Associate-Professor in Computer Science at INSAT Institute and Full Professor in computer Science in July 2018. From 2014 to August 2015, Abderrazak Jemai became the General Director (CEO) of the National Center of Informatics in Tunisia (CNI) dealing with government applications and related access services, data storage, and security (eGov, Intranet, etc.). He is the principal investigator for the "Arezzo 4.0: Industry 4.0, IoT, AI & Cybersecurity" project in SERCOM/EPT Laboratory in Tunis. His last interest is focused on Cybersecurity and its challenges when using IoT, Clouds, and Artificial Intelligence applications. This covers also the codesign of secured smart embedded systems and the deployment of Smart-IoT (Smart Internet of Things) devices in the context of Smart City, Cloud Computing, Smart-Industry (Industry 4.0), and Big Data, Security, Cybersecurity.
